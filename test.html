Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Helmets should be replaced every 3-5 years or sooner if they have been in a crash. Look for signs of wear such as cracks, loose straps, or degraded padding. If your helmet no longer fits securely or has been exposed to impact, it’s time for a new one.

My bike has been in storage. Is it safe to ride?

Before riding, inspect your bike for any signs of rust, worn-out tires, or loose components. Check the brakes, tire pressure, and lubricate the chain if necessary. If in doubt, take it to a bike shop for a safety check.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always follow traffic laws, ride in the same direction as traffic, and use hand signals when turning. Wear bright clothing, use lights at night, and stay aware of your surroundings. Prioritize safety by wearing a helmet and staying alert to road conditions.
